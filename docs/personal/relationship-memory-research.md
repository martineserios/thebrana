## Dunbar's Layers

Fractal structure with ~3x scaling:

| Layer | Size | Maintenance | Social time |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Inner 5** | 5 | Weekly (deep) | 10% |
| **Close 15** | 15 | Monthly+ | 60% |
| **Good 50** | 50 | 2x/year | 20% |
| **Known 150** | 150 | 1x/year | 10% |

Maintaining 150 relationships requires ~42% of total social time. The inner 5 + 15 consume disproportionate effort relative to size.

## Expert Practices

**Keith Ferrazzi ("Never Eat Alone"):**
- 80% of relationships = casual "pinging" (light maintenance)
- Real networking = making others successful, not transactional
- Connectors constantly give and receive

**Tim Ferriss:**
- One event per week/month with intent
- Research attendees beforehand
- Focus on ONE deep connection per event
- Quality over quantity; depth before breadth

## What to Track

**Minimum viable set:**
- How/where you met
- Key interests/passions
- Last interaction (date + summary)
- Commitments made ("said I'd send X")
- Important dates (birthday, etc.)

**High value if maintained:**
- Conversation history / meeting notes
- Shared projects or collaborations
- Family connections
- Communication preference (channel, cadence)

**High signal, rarely tracked:**
- Relationship trajectory (met → connected → deepened → dormant)
- Reciprocity balance (who initiates more?)
- Dunbar layer assignment

## File-Based Approach (Recommended for Solo Operator)

Markdown + Git-backed:
- Zero cost, full privacy, offline-first, version-controlled
- Pair with Dunbar framework: tag or folder by layer
- Dated interaction log entries, simple templates
- Migrate to Monica later if you want UI + reminders

## Privacy & Ethics

**The line:** Trust + transparency. If a contact asked "do you have notes on me?", would you hide them? If you'd show them and they'd understand, it's fine.

**Dunbar framing dissolves the creepiness:** You already remember things about people you care about. Writing it down isn't surveillance — it's externalizing memory you'd keep anyway. Ethics live in *why* you're tracking and *who sees it*.

## Patterns for Personal Space

| Pattern | Application | Priority |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Dunbar layer assignment | Categorize contacts into 5/15/50/150 | High |
| Ferrazzi's 80/20 pinging | Design for many light touches + few deep conversations | High |
| File-based (hal_md style) | Markdown contact files with interaction logs | High |
| Reciprocity awareness | Notice who initiates, who gives more | Medium |
| Trajectory tracking | Label relationship stage (new → deepening → stable → dormant) | Medium |

## What NOT to Steal

- Clay/Nection AI enrichment (overkill for solo operator)
- UpHabit (deprecated)
- Any tool that scrapes social media for contact data (creepy)

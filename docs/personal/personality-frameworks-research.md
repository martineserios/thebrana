# Personality Quantification & Self-Knowledge Frameworks

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Purpose:** Input for personal space design (input #8)

## Framework Comparison

| Framework | Purpose | Scientific validity | Time | Best for |
|-----------|---------|:------------------:|------|----------|
| **Big Five (OCEAN)** | Trait measurement | Very high | 5-15 min | Understanding behavioral baseline |
| **Schwartz Values** | Motivation mapping | High | 10-15 min | Clarifying what you actually care about |
| **VIA Strengths** | Strength inventory | High | 10-15 min | Building on what's working |
| **Enneagram** | Dynamic personality + growth | Low scientific, high resonance | 10 min | Predicting stress behavior |
| **MBTI** | Preference sorting | Low | 5-10 min | Fun conversation starter only |
| **Narrative Identity** | Story coherence | High (for well-being) | 10-20 min | Making sense of life trajectory |

## Big Five (OCEAN) — The Gold Standard

Five dimensions, all continuous (not binary):
- **Openness** — creativity, curiosity, intellectual engagement
- **Conscientiousness** — self-control, diligence, organization
- **Extraversion** — boldness, energy, social engagement
- **Agreeableness** — kindness, cooperation, empathy
- **Neuroticism** — anxiety, emotional reactivity (inverse = Emotional Stability)

**Why it matters:** Most validated personality model in psychology. Predicts real-world outcomes. Test-retest reliable.

**Free assessments:** [BigFive.ly](https://bigfive.ly/en) (5 min), [IPIP-BFM-20](https://openpsychometrics.org/tests/IPIP-BFFM/) (public domain, 20 items), [Truity](https://www.truity.com/test/big-five-personality-test).

**For identity design:** Map scores. Low Conscientiousness + want discipline? You need stronger systems/habits (you're working against baseline). High Openness + low Conscientiousness = you'll get bored with rigid plans.

## Schwartz Values (10 Types)

Arranged in a circular structure with two key tensions:

```
Openness to Change ←→ Conservation
Self-Enhancement  ←→ Self-Transcendence
```

1. Self-Direction (autonomy)
2. Stimulation (novelty)
3. Hedonism (pleasure)
4. Achievement (success)
5. Power (control)
6. Security (safety)
7. Conformity (obedience)
8. Tradition (customs)
9. Benevolence (helping close others)
10. Universalism (welfare of all)

**For identity design:** Choose which values matter. Conflicts between adjacent values (Stimulation vs Security) are where personal friction lives. If you value both Achievement and Benevolence, design systems that integrate them.

Validated across 80+ countries. Motivational, not behavioral — reveals what you *care about*.

## VIA Character Strengths (24 Strengths)

6 virtues, 24 strengths. Positive psychology — "strengthen strengths" not "fix weaknesses."

**Free assessment:** [viacharacter.org](https://www.viacharacter.org/) (10-15 min, no registration). Get ranked profile — top 5 are your "signature strengths."

**For identity design:** Build habits around your top 3. Someone high in "prudence" designing life around spontaneity will fail. Someone high in "creativity + love of learning" will struggle with routine unless they frame it as creative.

## Enneagram — Stress/Growth Dynamics

9 types, each with core fear, desire, and behavior under stress vs growth.

**Key insight:** It describes *dynamic* personality — how you change under pressure. Growth arrows point to the type whose positive traits you integrate. Stress arrows point to where you regress.

Example: Type 9 ("Peacemaker") → under stress becomes Type 6 (anxious). Under growth becomes Type 3 (assertive, goal-oriented).

**For identity design:** If you know your type + arrows, design preventive systems for stress patterns and cultivate growth-direction traits.

**Scientific validity:** Limited. But high personal resonance — people are more likely to act on it because it *feels* accurate.

## MBTI — Skip It

- 39-76% of people get different types when retaken after 5 weeks
- False dichotomy (you're either introverted OR extroverted — but traits are continuous)
- Doesn't predict job performance, leadership, or compatibility
- Use Big Five instead for anything serious

## Miessler's 12-Trait Framework

**Not a published framework.** His 12 traits (Enthusiasm, Energy, Directness, Precision, Curiosity, etc.) on 0-100 scales appear to be a personal invention for configuring AI personality, not a validated psychological model.

**What's useful:** The *practice* of deliberate quantification — choosing where you want to be on each dimension, not where you naturally fall. Prescriptive, not descriptive.

## Deliberate Identity Design

Three complementary models:

**Identity-Based Habits (James Clear):**
- Every action "votes" for the person you want to be
- ~51% consistency adopts a new identity
- "I am someone who exercises" > "I need to get fit"

**Growth Mindset (Carol Dweck):**
- Capacities develop through effort, not fixed
- Frame challenges as learning, not threats
- "I'm developing creativity" > "I'm not creative"

**Narrative Identity Theory:**
- You construct identity by integrating past, present, and imagined future into a coherent *story*
- Well-being correlates with narrative coherence
- Regularly rewrite your life story to guide future choices

## 30-Minute Self-Knowledge Exercise (Practical Minimum)

**Part 1 — Big Five (10 min):**
Take IPIP-BFM-20 or BigFive.ly. Note extremes (near 0 or 100).

**Part 2 — Values (10 min):**
Pick 5 from Schwartz's 10. Rank top 3. Where do they conflict?

**Part 3 — Identity Narrative (10 min):**
- "I am someone who ___" (5 sentences)
- "I want to become someone who ___" (5 sentences)
- What's the gap? What behaviors bridge it?

**Output:** Trait scores (baseline) + values hierarchy (drivers) + identity narrative (direction) + 1-3 behavior experiments.

## Patterns for Personal Space

| Pattern | Application | Priority |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Big Five baseline | Know your trait starting point | High |
| Values hierarchy (Schwartz) | Know what drives you | High |
| Identity narrative (quarterly update) | Coherent story → well-being | High |
| 30-min self-knowledge exercise | Low-effort, high-insight entry point | High |
| Deliberate trait quantification (Miessler-style) | Choose who to be, not just discover | Medium |
| Enneagram stress/growth arrows | Predict failure patterns | Medium |
| VIA signature strengths | Build on what works | Medium |

## What NOT to Steal

- MBTI (unreliable, skip entirely)
- 200-question assessments (overkill for design phase)
- Fixed identity labels ("I am an INTJ") — use continuous traits
- Miessler's specific 12 traits (not validated — design your own dimensions)

# Visual Style Guide: Minimalist / Modern

## Identity

Clean, contemporary, breathable. The kind of design that looks effortless but communicates clearly. LinkedIn-native — feels like it belongs in a premium feed. The visual equivalent of "simple systems, not simple problems."

## Visual Characteristics

- **Line quality:** Thin, consistent, clean. 1-2px weight. Feels light.
- **Shapes:** Simple rounded rectangles for components. Generous padding inside. No borders on some elements — just filled shapes with soft shadows.
- **Arrows:** Thin lines with small arrowheads. Subtle curves instead of sharp angles. Flow feels smooth.
- **Text:** Modern sans-serif (Inter, SF Pro, or similar feel). Generous spacing. Component names in medium weight, descriptions in light weight.
- **Colors:** White background. One primary color (deep teal, slate blue, or warm charcoal) for components. One accent color (coral, amber, or sage green) for highlights and key elements. Maximum 3 colors total.
- **Background:** Pure white or very light gray. No texture. Whitespace is a design element — generous margins.
- **Layout:** Centered, balanced. Vertical flow preferred (top to bottom). Plenty of space between components.

## How to Show Before/After

- **Before:** Components floating with space between them. No connecting lines — or very faint, dashed lines. Visual loneliness. The gap (silent bottleneck) is literal empty space with a subtle label.
- **After:** Same components now connected with solid lines. New components appear in the accent color. Everything flows continuously. The empty space is now filled with intentional connections.
- Split: left half "before" (muted colors), right half "after" (full colors). Or two separate slides in a carousel.

## Component Visual Language

- **The human gate:** A component with a small pause icon (||) or person silhouette. Slightly different shape (rounded circle vs rectangle) to stand out.
- **The silent bottleneck:** Empty space between two components. A tiny label in light gray: "40% lost here."
- **The memory layer:** A component with a subtle stack effect (two overlapping rectangles) suggesting depth/history.
- **The correction loop:** A curved arrow returning from bottom to top, in a lighter shade. Elegant, not busy.
- **The bridge:** A horizontal component that spans a subtle dividing line between "analog" and "digital" zones.

## Mood

Calm confidence. "I make complex things simple." Not flashy, not trying to impress — just clear. The design says: this person thinks clearly.

## LinkedIn Dimensions

- Carousel slide: 1080 x 1350 px (4:5 ratio, portrait)
- Text must be readable at mobile size — favor larger fonts over more detail
- Title at top with generous margin, diagram in center, "Todo es un sistema." at bottom in light weight
